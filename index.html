Railway Study: Advanced Dynamic SSI of Ballasted Railway Tracks over Heterogeneous Subgrade Stiffness Profiles
  • Developed a 3D plane strain FEM model in Abaqus CAE to simulate train-induced soil-track dynamic interaction.
  • Modeled subsoil using the Mohr-Coulomb constitutive model with consolidation effects for contrasting soil stiffness conditions.
  • Implemented infinite boundary conditions with spring-dashpot elements to simulate soil damping and wave energy dissipation.
  • Evaluated rail-top velocity response and subsoil displacement to characterize vibration propagation and soil-structure interaction.

  • Performed image preprocessing, augmentation, and feature enhancement using Python and OpenCV to improve input data quality.
  • Implemented efficient training pipelines with GPU acceleration and batch optimization to handle large-scale image datasets.
  • Evaluated models using accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score, ensuring reliable multi-label crack type classification.
Passenger Comfort: Passenger Vibration Perception Threshold Assessment in an immersive 4D Railway Simulation
  • Configured a vibrational seating system with VR interface by supplying controlled sinusoidal inputs to replicate rail-induced base excitations.
  • Acquired seat acceleration response using triaxial accelerometers and logged data for frequency-domain analysis.
  • Processed signals in MATLAB for noise removal, filtering, peak seat velocity computation, and transmissibility spectra.
  • Determined human perception thresholds across body regions within the 4–8 Hz critical range, referencing ISO 2631 comfort limits.
Structural Design: Structural Efficiency and Cost Benchmarking of G+6 Building under EN 1992 and IS 456:2000
  • Modeled a G+6 reinforced concrete structure in ETABS under IS 456:2000 and EN 1992 design provisions.
  • Computed dead and live loads as per codal guidelines and performed linear static and dynamic response analyses.
  • Observed IS 456:2000 to be cost-conservative with reduced strength and ductility, while EN 1992 ensured higher structural capacity.
  • Evaluated storey drift, lateral displacement, and load distribution to compare global structural performance under both codes.
